The dumbo octopus is the deepest living octopus, usually found between 3000 and 7000 meters beneath the ocean's surface. There are at least 15 species, all named after their large fins that resemble the ears of famous Disney character Dumbo the Elephant. They use these ear-like fins to slowly flap to move, while steering with their arms. Unlike many other octopus species, the dumbo octopus does not have an inc sac as it does not face the threat of predators as heavily as its surface counterparts. Instead, they have the ability to flush the transparent layer of their skin at will to blend in with their dark environment.
